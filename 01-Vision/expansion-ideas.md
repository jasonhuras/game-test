# Expansion & Creative Ideas

This document contains brainstormed ideas for future content, features, and expansions. Not all will be implemented, but they serve as inspiration and possibilities.

---

## Game Modes

### 1. Endless Mode
**Concept**: No wave cap, see how far you can go
**Features**:
- Infinite scaling enemies
- Leaderboard for highest wave
- Unique cosmetic rewards for milestones (Wave 50, 100, 200, etc.)
- Special "Endless-only" augments

**Why**: Appeals to hardcore players, content creators

---

### 2. Boss Rush Mode
**Concept**: Fight only bosses, back-to-back
**Features**:
- 10 bosses in a row
- Heal to full between bosses
- Choose 1 augment between each boss
- Leaderboard for fastest clear time

**Why**: Focus on skill, reduce downtime

---

### 3. Horde Mode
**Concept**: 10x enemy count, half health/damage
**Features**:
- Screen absolutely filled with enemies
- Focus on AoE builds
- Visual spectacle (thousands of kills)

**Why**: Power fantasy, satisfying for clips/streams

---

### 4. Curse Mode
**Concept**: Hard mode with modifiers
**Features**:
- Random curses applied each run (e.g., "Half health", "No healing", "Enemies move faster")
- Higher rewards (2x essence, unique unlocks)
- Bragging rights (special border for curse mode completions)

**Why**: Challenge for veterans

---

### 5. Daily Challenge Mode
**Concept**: Fixed seed, fixed augment pool
**Features**:
- Everyone gets same run (same spawns, same augments)
- Leaderboard for best performance
- Unique daily rewards

**Why**: Fair competition, watercooler moments ("Did you beat today's daily?")

---

### 6. Co-op Survival (PvE Multiplayer)
**Concept**: 2-4 players fight together
**Features**:
- Shared wave timer
- Friendly fire optional
- Revive system (dead players can be revived)
- Harder scaling (more enemies for more players)

**Why**: Social play, play with friends

---

### 7. Roguelike Campaign Mode
**Concept**: Multiple runs connected by story
**Features**:
- 5 "chapters", each with unique boss
- Carry over some power between chapters
- Story cutscenes
- Final ultimate boss

**Why**: Narrative structure, replayability

## Biome & Enemy Ideas

### Biome Concepts

**1. Crimson Forest** (Starting Biome)
- Theme: Dark, vampire-themed forest
- Enemies: Wolves, zombies, skeleton warriors
- Boss: Vampire Lord

**2. Frozen Wastes**
- Theme: Icy tundra, blizzards
- Enemies: Ice elementals, frost giants, yetis
- Hazard: Slow movement on ice
- Boss: Frost Dragon

**3. Volcanic Hellscape**
- Theme: Lava, fire, demonic
- Enemies: Fire imps, lava golems, demons
- Hazard: Fire pools that damage player
- Boss: Infernal Demon King

**4. Corrupted Jungle**
- Theme: Overgrown, poisonous plants
- Enemies: Venomous snakes, plant monsters, insects
- Hazard: Poison pools
- Boss: Venomous Hydra

**5. Celestial Realm**
- Theme: Holy, angelic, clouds
- Enemies: Corrupted angels, light constructs
- Hazard: Blinding light zones
- Boss: Fallen Archangel

**6. Void Dimension** (Endgame)
- Theme: Eldritch, cosmic horror
- Enemies: Void creatures, cosmic horrors
- Hazard: Reality tears (teleport randomly)
- Boss: Void God

---

### Unique Enemy Types

**1. Shielded Enemies**
- Front-facing shield blocks damage
- Must attack from behind or use shield-breaking abilities

**2. Teleporting Enemies**
- Blink around, hard to hit
- Require prediction or AoE

**3. Summoner Enemies**
- Spawn weaker enemies
- Priority target (kill summoner first)

**4. Healer Enemies**
- Heal nearby enemies
- Create strategic priority

**5. Bomber Enemies**
- Explode on death
- Risk-reward (kill them, but avoid explosion)

**6. Mimic Enemies**
- Look like pickups (gold, health)
- Surprise attack when approached

## Character Concepts (Future)

**1. Necromancer**
- Playstyle: Summon army of undead
- Passive: Enemies have 10% chance to rise as allied skeleton on death
- Starting Weapon: Necromancer Staff (summons minions)

**2. Berserker**
- Playstyle: High risk, high reward melee
- Passive: Gain damage when below 50% health
- Starting Weapon: Great Axe (slow, massive damage)

**3. Archer**
- Playstyle: Long-range precision
- Passive: Damage increases with distance from target
- Starting Weapon: Longbow (high damage, slow fire rate)

**4. Monk**
- Playstyle: Fast melee combos
- Passive: Every 3rd hit deals bonus damage
- Starting Weapon: Fists/Claws (very fast, low damage)

**5. Chronomancer**
- Playstyle: Time manipulation
- Passive: 10% chance to dodge by rewinding time
- Starting Weapon: Time Staff (slows enemies)

**6. Plague Doctor**
- Playstyle: Poison and debuffs
- Passive: All damage applies poison
- Starting Weapon: Plague Bomb Launcher (AoE poison)

**7. Paladin**
- Playstyle: Tank with support abilities
- Passive: Heal nearby allies (co-op mode)
- Starting Weapon: Holy Mace (melee, heals on hit)

**8. Shadow Assassin**
- Playstyle: Stealth and burst
- Passive: First hit from stealth is critical
- Starting Weapon: Dual Daggers (fast, crit-focused)

## Augment Ideas (Creative/Fun)

**1. Giant Mode** (Legendary)
- Player size +200%
- Health +100%
- Damage +50%
- Can't dodge (too big)

**2. Tiny Mode** (Legendary)
- Player size -50%
- Movement speed +50%
- Enemies have 20% chance to miss you

**3. Rewind** (Legendary)
- On death, rewind 10 seconds with full health
- Once per run

**4. Merchant's Greed** (Epic)
- Gold drops act as healing
- 1 gold = 1 HP

**5. Chaos** (Legendary)
- Every level, gain 3 random augments instead of choosing
- High variance, fun randomness

**6. Pacifist** (Curse)
- Can't deal damage
- Enemies die if they touch you
- 3x experience gain

**7. One Punch** (Legendary)
- All attacks deal 1 damage
- But every 100th hit deals 100,000 damage

**8. Mimic** (Epic)
- Copy the last augment you picked
- Get it twice

## Social Features

### Guild System
**Features**:
- Create/join guilds (up to 50 members)
- Guild leaderboard (combined progress)
- Guild challenges (all members contribute)
- Guild cosmetics (shared banner, spray)

**Why**: Social engagement, team goals

### Spectator Mode
**Features**:
- Watch friends' runs live
- Send emotes/messages
- Learn from better players

**Why**: Community, content creation

### Replay System
**Features**:
- Save and share replays
- Watch your best runs
- Share to social media

**Why**: Highlight moments, content

### Trading (Controversial)
**Features**:
- Trade cosmetics with friends
- Cannot trade power (characters, weapons, augments)
- Steam marketplace integration (if on Steam)

**Why**: Economy, player engagement

**Risk**: Exploits, black market. Consider carefully.

## Monetization Ideas (Ethical)

### Premium Battle Pass
**Cost**: $10 per season (3 months)
**Rewards**: Skins, emotes, sprays, weapon skins
**No P2W**: Cosmetic only

### Cosmetic Shop
**Items**: Character skins, weapon skins, emotes
**Currency**: Premium currency (purchased with real money)
**Earnable**: Some premium currency from free battle pass

### Supporter Packs
**Concept**: $20-50 bundles for dedicated fans
**Includes**: Exclusive skin, emote, title, currency, support developers

### DLC (Expansion Packs)
**Concept**: Major content updates ($10-15)
**Includes**:
- 2-3 new characters
- New biome with new enemies
- 20+ new augments
- New story campaign

**Why**: Sustainable development, fund future content

**Important**: Base game must feel complete. DLC is extra, not required.

## Accessibility Features

### Difficulty Options
- Easy Mode (enemies have -30% health/damage)
- Normal Mode (balanced)
- Hard Mode (enemies +50% stats, higher rewards)

### Colorblind Modes
- Deuteranopia, Protanopia, Tritanopia modes
- Adjust UI colors for visibility

### Control Remapping
- Fully rebindable keys
- Gamepad support
- Mouse sensitivity adjustments

### Gameplay Assists
- Auto-aim assist (for accessibility, not competitive advantage)
- Slow-motion option (reduce game speed 50%)
- Pause anytime (PvE only)

**Why**: Inclusive design, more players can enjoy

## Platform Expansion

### Console Ports
- PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch
- Controller-optimized UI
- Cross-save (play on PC, continue on console)

### Mobile (Controversial)
- Simplified controls (touch-based)
- Shorter runs (10 minutes instead of 30)
- Separate balance (mobile can't handle as many enemies)

**Risk**: Quality concerns, "mobile game" stigma

### Steam Deck Optimization
- Verified for Steam Deck
- UI scaling for small screen
- Performance optimization (30fps stable)

## Final Thoughts

Not all ideas here will (or should) be implemented. Use this as a creative springboard. Focus on:
1. **Core experience first**: Nail the base game
2. **Quality over quantity**: 10 great characters > 50 mediocre ones
3. **Community feedback**: Listen to players
4. **Sustainable development**: Don't burn out the team

**Prioritize ruthlessly. Scope creep kills games.**
